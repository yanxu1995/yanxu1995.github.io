Introductory concepts

  • Management of what?

    • organization is a coordinated group of people who function to achieve particular goals.
    • it exist because particular goals cannot achieve by individuals.

  • Manager

    • a manager is a person who plans, organizes, leads, and controls (POLC) the allocation of organizational resources in pursuit of organization goals.
    • directs a group of people.
    • allocate other organizational resources.
    • differ from individual contributors.

History of Management thought

  • Traditional viewpoints of management

    1. Bureaucratic management
      Focuses on detailed rules and procedures to guide the behavior of employees.
      Benefits: efficiency and consistency / best for routine tasks and standardized service.
      cost: no freedom and creativity / slow decision making.

    2. Scientific management

      Focuses on objective observation and experimentation to increase efficiency.
      Benefits: efficiency and productivity / best for routine tasks and standardized service.
      cost: neglect of social needs.

    3. Administrative management

      Focuses on application of specific management principles to achieve efficiency.
      Benefits: professionalization of managerial roles.
      cost: overemphasis on rational behavior of managers.

  • Behavioral (Human relations) viewpoints of management

    Focuses on dealing effectively with the human aspects of organization to increase team performance.
    people do not just work for money.

  • System viewpoints of management

    This management viewpoints stresses the need to consider the potential effects of local decisions on the whole organizational system.

  • Contingency viewpoints of management

    This management viewpoints suggests that management practices should be consistent with the specific requirements of the situation: external environment?, product/service?. HR capability?

  • Quality viewpoints of management

    Total quality management(TQM)
    Focuses on making quality values the driving force behind leadership and any initiative.

Dynamics of Leadership

  • Regardless of position or rank, anyone can be a leader.

  • There is formal and informal leadership.

  • There are many levels of leadership

    • group leader
    • functional leader
    • business-unit leader
    • corporate leader
    • …

  • There have been an evolution in leadership theories

    • trait theories
    • behavioral theories
    • contingency theories

  • trait theories

    • Trait theories focus on personality, social or intellectual traits that differentiate leaders from non leaders.
    • exp.charismatic, enthusiastic, self-confident, intelligent…
    • Limitation: cause and effect relationships are not clear.

  • behavioral theories

    • behavioral theories focus on specific behaviors that differentiate leaders from non leaders.
    • limitation: changes in situations are not considered.

  • contingency theories

    • pic

  • Other views

    • a transformational leader inspires by providing a vision, giving personal attention, and stimulating their intellectual capacity.
    • a transformational leader constantly tries to align the followers personal goals with the organizations goals

motivating employees

  • the concept of motivation
    • willingness to make some effort toward a goal, conditioned by the goals potential to satisfy some individual need.
  • theories of motivation

    • Needs

      • In organizations, it is important to understand the basic need of employees and satisfy them, to varying degrees, with specific motivational practices.

    • X or Y

      • X: the assumption that employees dislike work, and not responsible, and must be pushed to perform.
      • Y: the assumption that employees like work, and responsible, and perform well on their own.

    • Goal-setting

      • What, How much, when
      • specific

    • Expectancy

      • good - reward
      • bad - penalty

    • MAO

      • Motivation
      • Ability
      • Opportunity
